What this is

One system, read in four movements.

Bank and lender recovery in India is not governed by a single code. It is the interaction of four instruments that recognise, realise, adjudicate and resolve stressed debt. Read in isolation, each is a fragment; read together, they describe how a defaulted rupee travels from a lender's books to a court's order. These guides treat them as that single working machinery.

Layer 01 · Recognition

The operating system

The RBI Prudential Framework sits above the recovery statutes. It dictates when stress must be acknowledged, how a resolution plan must be attempted, and what provisioning bites when lenders delay.

Layer 02 · Realisation

The self-help remedy

SARFAESI lets a secured creditor seize and sell collateral on its own authority, without first knocking on the door of a court. Speed bought at the price of careful procedure.

Layer 03 · Adjudication

The specialised forum

The RDB Act built the Debts Recovery Tribunal, the dedicated forum for bank recovery that now also houses individual insolvency and hears appeals from SARFAESI action.

Layer 04 · Resolution

The corporate substrate

The Companies Act supplies the substantive corporate law — charges, directors' duties, schemes, fraud — that the Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code reads when the entity itself is restructured or resolved.

The Interface

How the four interlock.

A single default rarely lives inside one statute. The Prudential Framework forces the lender to act, SARFAESI lets it realise the security on its own steam, the RDB Act provides the tribunal where the borrower can be heard and the balance recovered, and the Companies Act, read with the IBC, restructures or resolves the whole entity when piecemeal recovery is not enough.

Recognise, realise, adjudicate, resolve. The same debt, four points of leverage.

I Recognition triggers the clockA missed payment crosses an RBI threshold; the lender must classify the stress and frame a resolution plan or face provisioning.
II Realisation without a courtIf security exists, SARFAESI lets the creditor issue notice, take possession and sell, subject to the borrower's statutory safeguards.
III Adjudication and the shortfallThe RDB Act's tribunal hears challenges to SARFAESI action and issues recovery certificates for any balance that remains.
IV Resolution of the entityWhen asset recovery falls short, the Companies Act and the IBC restructure or liquidate the corporate debtor itself, binding all stakeholders to the outcome.
