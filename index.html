What this is

One system, read in three movements.

Bank and lender recovery in India is not governed by a single code. It is the interaction of three instruments that recognise, realise and resolve stressed debt. Read in isolation, each is a fragment. Read together, they describe how a defaulted rupee travels from a lender's books to a court's order. These guides treat them as that single working machinery.

Layer 01 · Recognition

The operating system

The RBI Prudential Framework sits above the recovery statutes. It dictates when stress must be acknowledged, how a resolution plan must be attempted, and what provisioning bites when lenders delay.

Layer 02 · Realisation

The self-help remedy

SARFAESI lets a secured creditor seize and sell collateral on its own authority, without first knocking on the door of a court. Speed bought at the price of careful procedure.

Layer 03 · Adjudication

The specialised forum

The RDB Act built the Debts Recovery Tribunal, the dedicated forum for bank recovery that now also houses individual insolvency and hears appeals from SARFAESI action.

The Interface

How the three interlock.

A single default rarely lives inside one statute. The Prudential Framework forces the lender to act, SARFAESI lets it realise the security on its own steam, and the RDB Act provides the tribunal where the borrower can be heard and the balance recovered. The IBC then waits at the boundary of each.

Recognise, then realise, then adjudicate. The same debt, three points of leverage.

I Recognition triggers the clockA missed payment crosses an RBI threshold; the lender must classify the stress and frame a resolution plan or face provisioning.
II Realisation without a courtIf security exists, SARFAESI lets the creditor issue notice, take possession and sell, subject to the borrower's statutory safeguards.
III Adjudication and the shortfallThe RDB Act's tribunal hears challenges to SARFAESI action and issues recovery certificates for any balance that remains.
